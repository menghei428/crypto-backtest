A backtester asks: "If I had used this trading strategy in the past, how much money would I have made?" You're not predicting the future — you're testing a strategy on historical data to see if it has an edge. This is the most fundamental tool in quant trading.
The strategy we'll use is a Moving Average Crossover: buy when the short-term average price crosses above the long-term average (momentum is building), sell when it crosses back below. Simple, but it's the perfect teaching vehicle.

A Simple Moving Average (SMA) is just the average closing price over the last N days. It "smooths" out noise.

Short SMA (20 days) reacts quickly — it follows recent price action closely.
Long SMA (50 days) reacts slowly — it shows the bigger trend.

The signal: When the fast (20d) line crosses above the slow (50d) line, it means momentum is picking up → BUY. When the fast crosses below → momentum is fading → SELL.

Hidden assumption
This model assumes:

- No transaction costs
- Instant execution
- No slippage
- Perfect fills (buy or sell exactly at the price you intended)
